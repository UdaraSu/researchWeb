01 — Introduction

Project scope

Museums serve as custodians of memory and meaning. This study asks how digital products can extend that mission—especially for diverse audiences and resource-conscious institutions.

Literature survey

Contemporary literature on digital strategy in museums points to mobile interpretation, layered narratives, and optional personalization as drivers of satisfaction and learning. Visitors increasingly expect continuity between discovery online and depth on-site.

Work on inclusive design stresses captioned media, logical structure, legible type, and resilient layouts across devices. Lightweight feedback channels allow curatorial teams to refine content with evidence rather than assumption.

02 — Position

Research gap

◇

Fragmented journeys

Ticketing, wayfinding, and storytelling are often split across tools, producing friction before a visitor ever reaches a gallery.

◎

Local context

Few published frameworks adapt global UX patterns to regional languages, bandwidth, and staffing realities.

▣

Measurable engagement

Institutions need ethical, practical signals about which stories land—beyond raw download counts.

Research problem & solution

How might we design a digital companion that improves discovery, accessibility, and engagement for museum visitors?

Proposed solution: A standards-minded web experience for exhibit discovery, optional guided paths, accessible summaries, and shareable highlights—optimized for visitors’ own devices and extendable with lightweight curator workflows.

Discovery

Structured navigation and search-aware entry points into collections.

Access

Semantic HTML, contrast-aware theming, and resilient responsive layouts.

Insight

Privacy-conscious analytics hooks for iterative content improvement.
